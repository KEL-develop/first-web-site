"The Masterplan" is a compilation album by Oasis, released in 1998. It's a collection of B-sides, rare tracks, and previously unreleased songs from the band's prolific period between 1994 and 1997. The album features a mix of energetic B-sides like "Acquiesce," "Fade Away," and "Rockin' Chair," showcasing Oasis's signature anthemic sound. "Talk Tonight" and "Half the World Away" reveal a more introspective and acoustic side of the band, highlighting Noel Gallagher's songwriting versatility. A notable inclusion is Oasis's cover of The Beatles' "I Am the Walrus," performed live at London's legendary Abbey Road Studios. The track captures the band's admiration for their musical influences. The album's title track, "The Masterplan," stands out as a previously unreleased gem. Its sweeping melody and powerful lyrics make it a fan favorite and underscore the band's ability to craft impactful songs beyond their main studio albums. "The Masterplan" serves as a testament to Oasis's prolific creativity during a peak period in their career, offering fans a comprehensive collection of their lesser-known but equally impressive musical endeavors.
